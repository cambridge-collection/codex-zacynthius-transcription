From Severus the Archbishop
76 Let us acknowledge that the word of God has ineffably descended from the heavens, and from the Holy Spirit and from the
very substance of the virgin he has taken flesh, apart from turning and without
sin, and has become human for ever, as Paul says:
›“ When the fullness of time came, God sent his son,
› born from a woman. ” For on one hand, the conception, the birth, the swaddling clothes make trustworthy
the truth of the incarnation, on the other, the star appears to the magi from above, on a
bodiless path. Frankincense indicates his divinity, as it is carried towards God.
Gold bears witness to power for God over all things, as it lies under the feet of the king.
The fragrant myrrh[. . . . . .] indicates the salvific suffering for which he was made to exist in flesh
