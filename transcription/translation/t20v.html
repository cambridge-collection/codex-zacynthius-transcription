83 On the fortieth day there was a purification according to the law, because the shaping of
the male also takes forty days. This is a sign that the formation itself
required cleansing from God. The formation of Christ was holy: it was not from human
seeds but from divine power. His birth was also holy, but it was not
according to what had been made holy. The clean one was not cleansed. And just as he was baptised
when he did not need cleansing through water, but so that he should make the waters holy, and so that
he should fulfil all righteousness, in obedience to the prophet, so in this way for his purification
the offering was made for the one who did not require purification, doves and pigeons,
a sign of his simple and modest behaviour. Such things are shadows and models
for humans, but for Christ they are the truth. For he is the power of true modesty,
and the substance of true simplicity. ❧     From an unattributed source. ❧
