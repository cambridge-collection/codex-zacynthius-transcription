having neither gold or silver in their belts, or two tunics, that they must endure the
greatest hardship. This is a burden for those in toil and persecutions, and for this
reason and very appropriately, the one who knows the heart does not permit us to
be dispirited at what results from poverty. For, he says, those who are now hungry
for the sake of piety towards him will be satisfied, meaning that they will luxuriate
in the good things which will be given to them, plainly both the intelligible and
