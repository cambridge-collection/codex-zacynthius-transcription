From the same
81￣ The blessed disciples were going to be initiators and teachers of what was under the Heaven.
Accordingly they should display to people everywhere that they are perfectly suited to
piety: the way of a pure constitution needed to be known. They were craftsmen working
towards every good work and to implant the accurate and saving word which
led to truth in those who were instructed. They had already seen this before,
and had their intelligence richly illuminated by the divine light,
so that they should not be blind guides of the blind. And if it were to happen that
some of them did advance to this, so that they became a rival (εισαμιλλον) to those training to obtain
virtue, they will be set up as a measure of the teachers and they will be imitators
of them. Again, Paul is trustworthy when he says, “ Become imitators of me,
just as I am of Christ” , of the teacher who does not yet judge. Why do you yourself
judge? For he did not come to judge the world but to pity it. ❧
