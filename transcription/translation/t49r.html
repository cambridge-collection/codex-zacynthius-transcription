4￣ You asked about* the narrative of the holy gospels. You might inquire whether
the woman who anointed the Lord with myrrh was a single person or two, for the four evangelists
have written that he was anointed with myrrh by a woman. I have considered this myself
and made a careful study of the narrative. Having looked intently at the
accuracy of the words of the gospels, I reckon there are three women
and one man, from the quality of the characters and from the manner of the deed
itself and from the difference of the occasions. For John says this about Mary,
the sister of Lazarus and Martha, who is exceedingly praised and pious,
and is reckoned among the disciples for Jesus, giving his account as
follows: “ And so Jesus, six days before the Passover, came to Bethany, where there was
› Lazarus, whom Jesus raised from the dead. So they made a meal there for him.
› Martha waited at table, while Lazarus was one of those who reclined with him.
