5 It is written in Exodus that “the people saw the voice of the Lord,” * even though a voice is not seen
but heard. This was to show paradoxically that the voice of God is seen by those with the capacity to
see. In the gospel, however, it is not the voice which is seen but a word, which is greater than the voice.
This is why it says “Just as they were handed on to us by those who from the beginning were eyewitnesses
and servants of the word.” * For if seeing the Lord in bodily form made someone an eyewitness of this,
Pilate was also an eyewitness of the Word when he condemned him, and Judas who betrayed him, and
everyone who cried out “Crucify, crucify him.” * Seeing God’s word is understood where the Saviour
said: “The one who has seen me has seen the Father who sent me.” * And, having grasped what a
fine lesson this is, Luke teaches us next that contemplation is the purpose of some things seen, while
