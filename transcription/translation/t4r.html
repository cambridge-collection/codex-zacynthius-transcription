From Origen


5 It is written in Exodus that the people saw the sound of the Lord, even though a sound is not seen
but heard. This was to show paradoxically that the sound of God is seen by those to whom it is seen. In
the gospel, however, the sound is not seen but the greater word of the sound. For this reason, it was just as
those who became eyewitnesses and servants of the word from the beginning handed down to us. For if seeing
the Lord in bodily form made someone a eyewitness of this, Pilate was also an eyewitness of
the word when he condemned him, and Judas his betrayer, and everyone who shouted“ Crucify,
crucify him. ” So the knowledge of the word was perceived there, where the Saviour said: “ The one who has seen
me has seen the Father who sent me. ” And, having forgotten what a fine lesson this is, Luke
teaches us next that contemplation is the goal of some of what is seen, and that
