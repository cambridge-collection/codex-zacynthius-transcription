From Saint Cyril

19￣ For there are people who have faith within themselves in an uncontrived way, as if in the simplicity
of words, but their mind has not penetrated to the touchstone of the mystery. These
people have a piety towards God which is light and without roots. They go into the
churches, they join in with the multitude of those who are assembled and they gladly
accept initiation into the mysteries, but in an undiscriminating way and from shallow
desires. Once they have gone out of the churches, straightaway they are carried off to
forgetfulness of the holy teachings. While Christian matters are borne with
a fair wind, and there is no persecution oppressing them, they retain
such scarce faith as they have within them. But when oppression is raging,
their heart prefers conflict avoidance and their mind is fugitive. To them the prophet
