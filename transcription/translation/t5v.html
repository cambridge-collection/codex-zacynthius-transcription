13 Perhaps someone will ask why the word “blameless” is applied to them. For it would have been sufficient
for them to have walked in all the ways of God “and in righteousness”, unless it is possible to “walk in all
the commandments” * yet not without blame. How, then, someone says, is it possible to “walk in all
the commandments” not without blame? I say to them that, if this were not the case, it
would not have said, “Follow justly what is just.” * So whenever we observe the commandments,
there should not be in our conscience the filth of vanity or sycophancy or anything else
of this sort as we observe the commandments without blame.❧
