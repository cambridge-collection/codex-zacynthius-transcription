From Saint Severus of Antioch, from the Response against the
Apology of Julian, Chapter*

52￣ For it was he himself who also in the beginning made the heaven and the earth, and said: “ Let the earth
produce grassy plants; sow seed in its nature and similarity. ” * By this word, he put the power of
multiplication into each each seed, right up even to the fact that one seed
of mustard bears one hundredfold many times and everything else is fulfilled likewise. For this was
also granted to Isaac through his blessing. The holy writing bears witness; examine it
as follows: “ Isaac sowed in that land and found in that year that the
barley produced one-hundredfold: God blessed him. ” * So the one who multiplies
the seeds as both creator and God is not such that the mind of any Human could approach him.
Thus too is it more fitting to the divine and unexpected by far more than the middle that in the
five breads he
fulfils both
blessing and
power. ❧

In addition to that, nevertheless, as I am present, because your statement is the same as the teachings of the
holy fathers in leading towards God like in the completion of the mysteries. Let what stands for Jesus
be in the middle. Firstly, on the green grass of educational and improving thoughts
he lays you all down on the ground in groups of one hundred and groups of
fifty, just as Mark narrates. Next, they feed the five breads to you,
the words which are more hollow than the divine teachings, which are the purifications
of the five senses. They have now come for a perfect understanding, as if in
future the perceptions are kept stripped. They give the εστιασιν of the seven loaves,
which is the most perfect teaching, bearing in this“ the Spirit of wisdom and understanding,
the Spirit of advice and strength, the Spirit of knowledge and piety, the Spirit of the fear of God, ” *
just as Isaiah the prophet enumerated it. For the number seven is the token of perfection.
This is the reason why, after the five loaves, Jesus also set the table of the seven.
