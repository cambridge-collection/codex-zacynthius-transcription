From Saint Cyril

53￣ You see the skill of the inquiry. He did not immediately say, “ You, who do you say that I am? ”
Rather, he refers back to the report of those who were outside, that having rejected it and
shown it to be disreputable, he might shepherd them in future to the true opinion. This is indeed
what he did. For when the disciples said, “ Some say John the Baptist,
others Elijah, others that a prophet from those of the ancients has
arisen, ” he said to them, “ But you, who do you say that I am? ” Ah, how important is the“ You” ! It places them apart from the others, so that they might escape the
suspicions of such people and not have a small opinion about him. He says“ You” ,
the chosen ones, who have been called to apostleship by the choice in me,
who are witnesses of the miracles I have wrought. “ Whom do you say I am? ” But again,

The disciple is unerring, for he does not then say simply that he is Christ from God, but rather
the Christ. For there are very many people who have been called christs from their anointing
by God in different ways. Some were anointed to be rulers,
others prophets. Other have also received redemption through him, the Christ, the Saviour
of us all, meaning that we have been anointed with the holy Spirit
and have taken on the calling of Christ. Accordingly, there are very many christs, but they are called
this after the deed. there is one and only one who is from the God and Father. It is not as if
we are truly christs, nor rather those of God, but of someone else: he and he alone,
however, has as Father the one in the Heavens. Therefore it is clear
that, distinguishing him from the multitude of others, Peter related him to the Father, as
being his alone. He was God by nature and shone forth unspeakably from God the Father; his
only-begotten word became flesh according to what is written. ❧
