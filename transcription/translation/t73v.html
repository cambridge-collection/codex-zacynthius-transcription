33 On the Man who was not Permitted to Follow. ❧
*72 From Saint Cyril
To desire the gifts from above from God is truly worth acquiring, and it
would be a cause of all good. Accordingly, the master of all gives as a generous
giver, not simply to all in an indiscriminate way, but especially to those who would
be worthy to receive them. If, then, someone should wish to be considered worthy
of such gifts, let them deliver their soul from the stains leading to meanness and causes
for blame: thus they will be acceptable. But let the one who is not like this in their inclination
depart a long way off. * And a little later on He justly made him to be sent away. For he
should have taken up his own cross, meaning to die to the world or rather to
renounce the empty distractions of this present life. For the friendship of the world is
the enmity of God. As the man was this sort of person, Jesus examines him obliquely, not
reproaching him but rather
correcting him so that of his own
accord he might become better.

wicked, cunning and resisting powers, which are the herds of demons,
foxes and birds of heaven. For they are called this in very many places
by the divinely-inspired scripture. The blessed Psalmist said about such people:
› “They will be the portions of foxes.” * In the Song of Songs it is written:
› “Catch for us the little foxes that wipe out the vines.” * And Christ himself
says about Herod somewhere, “Say to that fox.” * And he also said somewhere about
the seeds which fell on the earth, that “The birds of the heaven came and
ate them up.” * We claim that he did not say this about perceptible and
visible birds, but rather about unclean and wicked spirits.
