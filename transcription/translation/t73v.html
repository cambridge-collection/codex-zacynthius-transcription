72 From Saint Cyril
To desire the gifts from above from God is truly worth acquiring, and it
would become the cause of all good. Accordingly, the master of all gives as
a generous giver, not simply to all in an indiscriminate way, but especially to those
who should be worthy to receive them. If, then, an old man should wish to be considered worthy
of such things, let him put away from his soul the dirt which leads to meanness
and accusations. For thus he will be acceptable. But let the one who does not have such an opinion
depart a long way off. And a little later on He justly made him to be sent away. For he should have
taken up his own cross, meaning to die to the world. He was considering
renouncing the empty distractions of this present life: for the friendship of the
world is the enmity of God. As the Human was of this nature, Jesus examines him obliquely, rather than
reproaching him. He
