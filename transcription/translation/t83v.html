From Saint Severus, Archbishop of Antioch, from Sermon 89

100￣ Christ deliberately called himself the Samaritan for the present purpose. For since
he was having a conversation with the lawyer, who bragged greatly with regard to the law,
he was keen to set him in his place by what was said. So not the priest, not the Levite, not( to put
it simply) those who thought that they behaved according to the laws of Moses, but it was he who came
to fulfil the intention of the law. He revealed by his very works who is the neighbour,
and what it means to love them as oneself. The Jews said of him in scorn, “ You
are a Samaritan and you have a demon” * and they accused him very frequently of breaking
the law. ❧ And a little later on. ￣ The Samaritan on his journey, who is Christ, saw the man lying
