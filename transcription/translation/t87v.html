12￣ He wishes his own disciples to be kind and not to hold a grudge, so that they are able
to say blamelessly in their prayers, “ Forgive us our sins, because we
too forgive everyone who owes to us. ” What depth of wealth and wisdom and
knowledge! First he instructs them to ask forgiveness of the sins which have been committed, and then
to confess* in this way that they themselves too will forgive completely and utterly and, if I may say so,
they wish to become an imitator of God in the forbearance which is within them. And just as they were able
to show kindness to their fellow servants, they seek to receive in equal measure
from him who dispense what is righteous and knows to have pity on all people. And
