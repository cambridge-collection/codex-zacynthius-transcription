The person who encounters this book ought to know that it comes from many
works of holy and orthodox fathers: and not only from these, but also from discredited
exegetes. And when they encounter the section of the heretics
notes are set down there which are for avoiding, as there are teachings in them
unharmonious with church tradition, having been spoken by the
heretics. And I did not do this of my own accord, having followed
our most holy father, the archbishop of the great and Christ-loving city
of Alexandria, Cyril, who says in his Letter to Eulogius:
› One ought not to avoid and refuse everything which the heretics say.
› For they grant many things which we also grant. And I thought
that it was indispensable to add this to the present introduction as a clarification
and distinction for those who encounter it. For they should know that those
who have interpreted God-inspired scripture have not only
been carried in different ways in their ideas
but, indeed, have not divided the very words of the same divine
scripture identically, and have contributed their interpretation in this manner. For
they have made their exposition with one of them combining more words and another fewer:
the numbers which are assigned to the chapters are an indication of this,
to provoke those who read the text to go further forwards.
Accordingly, one ought to read the first and second and even third chapter
of the text of the divine scripture, and the accompanying interpretation in this manner
so that the ideas are easily taken in for those who encounter them.
