From Saint Cyril ❧

42 See how he lodges with one of the disciples, a human who was poor and indistinguished
in his way of life, yet he willingly endures as he dwells with us, so that we might
be enriched by his poverty and we might learn how to sympathise with the
meek and not to despise those who are in need and distress. Accordingly,
let us too welcome Jesus, for whenever he enters and we have him among us
in our mind and heart, then he will quench the fever of unsuitable
pleasures. But he also raises up those who are strong, he appears manifestly in a spiritual way,
so that they also minister to him, meaning that they fulfil what is appropriate for him.
Observe, too, for me, how much benefit the touch of his holy flesh possesses.
