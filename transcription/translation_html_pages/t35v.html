72 He calls blessed those who weep and says that they will laugh. But we say that weeping
is not simply the teardrop which comes from the eyes, for this is common and
characteristic of all, believers and unbelievers, if something were to come to pass of the customary things which cause
grief. Rather it is those who shun a life which is joyful and refined and among
luxuries, meaning fleshly things. For we say that these are in rejoicing and
laughter, but the others who decline luxurious living and fleshly propensities
and weep all night because they hate what is in the world, these the Saviour says are
blessed. Accordingly, having commanded us to be poor, he garlands with honours the things which follow
poverty. These are the lack of necessities for nourishment, and being downcast through
affliction. For it is written that“ The afflictions of the righteous are many, and the Lord
will save them from all of them. ” ❧           From the same ❧
