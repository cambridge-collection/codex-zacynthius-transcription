From an unattributed source

41￣ It was not possible to draw close to the Lord and to touch him bodily, once one had drawn near
and touched, unless one were to do this spiritually. It happened, however, that this was what
the woman did, which is the reason that she is sought by the Lord. He calls the disciples to his presence, even
though they do not know what is being sought, but think that the Lord is speaking about a single
touch, of the same nature as all the others touched. For this reason, the Lord explains
and says, “ Someone touched me” , just as he also said“ The one who has ears to hear, let them hear” even though
everyone has this sort of bodily hearing and all hear up to the bare
hearing of the words. But it was neither possible to hear what was not intelligible,
nor to touch what was not credible. So why does he call into his presence and raise his holy
eyes to inspect the woman? Because she is worthy of the Lord’s sight. She is also worthy of
demonstration to those who are present, because she would be an example of faith to all
and an encouragement to faith through the praise which would rightly be given to her. ❧
From Saint Cyril
41 So did the Lord in his
desire for fame not allow
to go unnoticed the display
of divine activity or the unexpected event which happened to the woman? We do not
claim this, but rather the following. He is everywhere looking to the benefit of those who are called
to grace through faith. Accordingly, while the sign which is not recognised has done wrong to
many, it has benefitted in no small way the sort of person who is familiar and above all the leader
of the synagogue. For he began to set his hope more securely on what was
expected, and he granted stronger courage because Christ would also rescue his daughter
from the snares of death. So we know that God is Emmanuel and does not need eyes which
see to understand everything, nor does he have a perceptible understanding
of affairs, but it is beyond bodily perception. ❧
From Saint Severus, Archbishop of Antioch, from Sermon 51
41￣ He inquired“ Who touched me? ” not because he did not know the woman who touched him, but because
he wished through the inquiry to make public the touch which was only noticed by him. Of course,
the disciples were also at a loss. For there were many people who were pressing hard, or indeed
touching, to say it properly. He called out, “ Someone touched me. For I realised that power
had gone out of me. ” The power which he said had gone out was the activity of healing which
she had drawn out by faith and the cures which she had drawn up as if from a flowing well. ❧
